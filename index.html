Global Economic Factors

Welcome to my data visualization website, where we explore the intricate dynamics of global economic factors. Through engaging charts and graphs, we analyze crucial metrics such as world GDP, age dependency ratios, migration trends, and carbon emissions.



GDP data around the world

One of the most important economic indicators of a country is the Gross Domestic Product or GDP of the country, and it is one of the most commonly used measure of a country's economic performance.

Source: ourworldindata.org



GDP vs Government Expenses around the world

Even if a nation has an extremely high GDP, it is important to understand how much of that GDP is being spent by the government. If the government is spending a large portion of the GDP, then it could be an indicator of an unhealthy economy. Therefore, it is important to understand the relationship between these factors

Source: ourworldindata.org



Net Migration by Country

The amount of migrants coming into a nation is also an extremely good indicator of how healthy the country's economy is. In general, a nation with a high number of migrants coming in tends to indicate that the country has a booming economy, thus providing job opportunities for immigrants. However, it is also important to consider the number of people moving out of the country. Therefore, we instead consider the net migration rate, which is the difference between these 2 indicators.

Source: ourworldindata.org



Ratio of Elderly Citizens to Working Age Citizens

One of the ways to predict the future state of a country is to see what proportion of its population consists of elderly citizens. These citizens will no longer be working, and will therefore be relying on the working age population. An extremely high ratio will indicate that the nation may require more foreign workers to fill the working roles.

Source: ourworldindata.org



Carbon Emission per Capita

Finally, we look at the carbon emissions per capita of a country. This is an important indicator of how much a country is contributing to global warming. The data must be normalised by population as otherwise, it would be unfair to countries with large populations. By visualising this, we are able to see the trend of each country to see how their C02 output has been over the last 40 years.

Source: ourworldindata.org
