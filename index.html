Global Economic Factors

Welcome to my data visualization website, where we explore the intricate dynamics of global economic factors. Through engaging charts and graphs, we analyze crucial metrics such as world GDP, age dependency ratios, migration trends, and carbon emissions.



GDP data around the world

One of the most important economic indicators of a country is the Gross Domestic Product or GDP of the country, and it is one of the most commonly used measure of a country's economic performance.

Source: ourworldindata.org

GDP data around the world

The map below shows the GDP of each nation around the world in terms of Billion USD$. You may hover over each nation to see the exact GDP value. This data is taken based on the 2022 GDP standings of the nation.

GDP vs Government Expenses (Billion USD$)

The scatter plot below shows the GDP of each nation in terms of Billion USD$ and the government expenses in terms of Billion USD$. You may hover over each point to see the exact GDP and government expenses value. This data is taken based on the 2022 GDP and government expenses of the nation. Hovering over each point will also show the trend of the nation's data over time. The size of each point will also show the ratio of a nation's Expense to its GDP.

Net Migration (Per 1000 people)

The bar chart shows important information on the net number of people migrating into a nation (normalised per 1000 people). This gives a good indication of which nations have more people coming into the country over time.

Proportion of Elderly Citizens to the Working Population

The information below gives an indication as to how mature a nation is. A nation which has an extremely high ratio of elderly citizens to the working population generally indicates that it is much more mature, meaning they may require more foreign workers immigrating into the nation to fill working roles.
